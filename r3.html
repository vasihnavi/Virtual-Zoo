Iguana

Iguana iguana

Did You Know?

Iguanas can detach their tails to escape predators!

They have a third "eye" on their heads for sensing light and movement.

Some iguanas can hold their breath for up to 30 minutes underwater.

Green iguanas are excellent swimmers and use their long tails to propel themselves.

Their sharp teeth are used to shred plant material.

Iguanas can change their body color to regulate temperature or for camouflage.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Green iguanas grow up to 5-6 feet long, including their tail.
Weight: Can weigh up to 11 pounds.
Features: Scaly skin, long tails, spines along their backs, and a dewlap under their chin.
Color: Usually green but may vary to brown or orange.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Herbivores, primarily eating leaves, fruits, and flowers.
Behavior: Forages during the day and relies on sharp teeth for chewing tough plant material.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Tropical rainforests, mangroves, and riverside areas.
Range: Found in Central and South America and some Caribbean islands.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: Live up to 15-20 years in captivity; slightly less in the wild.
Reproduction: Females lay 20-70 eggs in burrows; eggs hatch after 90 days.
Behavior: Hatchlings are independent from birth and must fend for themselves.
