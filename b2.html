Scarlet Macaw

Ara macao

Did You Know?

Scarlet Macaws can live up to 50 years in the wild.

They are among the most intelligent birds, capable of mimicking sounds and solving puzzles.

They communicate using loud squawks and screeches.

Their vibrant feathers help them camouflage in tropical rainforests.

They have a powerful beak that can crack hard nuts and seeds.

Scarlet Macaws mate for life and share strong bonds with their partners.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: About 2.5 feet in length, with a wingspan of up to 3.5 feet.
Weight: 2-3 pounds.
Features: Vibrant red feathers with blue and yellow on wings; strong, curved beak.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Fruits, seeds, nuts, and nectar.
Feeding: Uses strong beak to crack hard seeds and nuts.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Tropical rainforests.
Range: Found in Central and South America.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: 40-50 years in the wild.
Reproduction: Lays 2-4 eggs; incubated for 24-28 days.
