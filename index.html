Animals

Mammals

Lion

Scientific Name: Panthera leo

Fun Fact: Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides.

Giant Panda

Scientific Name: Ailuropoda melanoleuca

Fun Fact: Giant pandas spend about 12 hours a day eating bamboo, which makes up 99% of their diet.

Asian Elephant

Scientific Name:Elephas maximus

Fun Fact: Asian elephants are smaller than African elephants but have larger ears and are known for their intelligence and strong family bonds.

Chimpanzee

Scientific Name: Pan troglodytes

Fun Fact:Chimpanzees share about 98% of their DNA with humans, making them one of our closest relatives.

Giraffe

Scientific Name: Giraffa camelopardalis

Fun Fact: :Giraffes are the tallest land animals, with some males growing up to 18 feet tall.

Birds

Bald Eagle

Scientific Name: Haliaeetus leucocephalus

Fun Fact: The bald eagle, a symbol of freedom, can spot prey from over a mile away thanks to its incredible eyesight.

Scarlet Macaw

Scientific Name: Ara macao

Fun Fact: Scarlet macaws can live up to 50 years and are known for their ability to mimic sounds, including human speech.

Emperor Penguin

Scientific Name: Aptenodytes forsteri

Fun Fact: Emperor penguins are the tallest and heaviest of all penguin species, and they can dive up to 1,800 feet in search of food.

Peacock

Scientific Name: Pavo cristatus

Fun Fact: Male peacocks fan out their stunning iridescent tail feathers to attract females during courtship displays.

Flamingo

Scientific Name: Phoenicopterus roseus

Fun Fact: Flamingos get their pink color from their diet, which is rich in beta-carotene found in algae and crustaceans.

Reptiles

Komodo Dragon

Scientific Name: Varanus komodoensis

Fun Fact: Komodo dragons are the largest lizards on Earth and can take down prey much larger than themselves thanks to their venomous bite.

Turtle

Scientific Name: Testudines

Fun Fact: Turtles have been around for over 200 million years, and some species can live over 100 years.

Iguana

Scientific Name: Iguana iguana

Fun Fact: Iguanas have a third eye on their heads called the parietal eye, which helps detect changes in light and shadows.

American Alligator

Scientific Name: Alligator mississippiensis

Fun Fact: American alligators have been around for over 200 million years, making them living dinosaurs.

Leopard Gecko

Scientific Name: Eublepharis macularius

Fun Fact: Leopard geckos are one of the few gecko species that have eyelids and can close their eyes completely.
