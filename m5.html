Asian Elephant

Elephas maximus

Did You Know?

Asian elephants are smaller than their African counterparts!

They have a single "finger" on the tip of their trunk for picking up objects.

Asian elephants are highly social and live in matriarchal herds.

They can communicate through low-frequency sounds and vibrations.

Their tusks are found only in males and a few rare females.

Asian elephants are excellent swimmers!

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Smaller than African elephants, standing up to 10 feet tall.
Weight: 2,000 to 5,000 kg (4,400 to 11,000 lbs).
Distinctive Features: Grey skin with depigmented patches; a single "finger" on the trunk.
Tusks: Found only in some males and rare females.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Herbivores, consuming grasses, leaves, fruits, and bark.
Feeding: Spend up to 16 hours a day feeding and consume up to 150 kg (330 lbs) of food.
Water: Drink large amounts daily from rivers and lakes.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Found in forests, grasslands, and swamps.
Geography: Native to India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia (Thailand, Myanmar, etc.).
Conservation: Classified as Endangered due to habitat loss and human conflict.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: Live up to 60 years in the wild.
Reproduction: Gestation lasts 22 months; typically one calf is born.
Social Structure: Live in matriarchal herds, with males becoming solitary as they mature.
