Lion

Panthera leo

Did You Know?

Lions are the only cats that live in groups, called prides!

A male lion's mane makes them appear larger and more intimidating.

Lions can run up to speeds of 50 miles per hour in short bursts.

They have a roar that can be heard from 5 miles away!

Lions sleep for up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy for hunting.

Lions are apex predators at the top of the food chain in their habitat.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Males weigh 330 to 550 lbs, females weigh 250 to 400 lbs.
Distinctive Features: Males have a large, mane of fur surrounding their head and neck.
Color: Lions have a tawny yellow or golden coat.
Claws: Retractable claws that help in hunting.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Carnivorous, primarily hunting large mammals like wildebeest, zebras, and buffalo.
Feeding: Lions hunt in groups; females typically do the hunting.
Water: Lions drink water regularly but can go without it for a few days if needed.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Grasslands, savannas, open woodlands, and deserts.
Geography: Native to sub-Saharan Africa, with a small population in the Gir Forest of India.
Conservation: Classified as Vulnerable due to habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: Live up to 10 to 14 years in the wild.
Reproduction: Females give birth to 1 to 4 cubs after a gestation period of 110 days.
Social Structure: Live in prides, with one or two males and several females.
