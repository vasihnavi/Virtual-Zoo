Komodo Dragon

Varanus komodoensis

Did You Know?

Komodo Dragons are the largest lizards in the world!

They can detect prey from up to 2.5 miles away using their strong sense of smell.

Their saliva contains venom, which helps them take down large prey.

They can consume up to 80% of their body weight in one meal.

Komodo Dragons can run up to 20 km/h (12 mph) in short bursts.

Females can reproduce without a mate through parthenogenesis.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Up to 10 feet long.
Weight: Up to 70 kg (154 lbs).
Features: Rough skin, sharp claws, and a muscular tail.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Carnivorous, feeding on deer, pigs, water buffalo, and carrion.
Feeding: Use sharp teeth and venom to subdue prey.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Dry tropical forests, savannas, and grasslands.
Range: Found only on a few Indonesian islands, including Komodo and Flores.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: 20-30 years in the wild.
Reproduction: Females lay up to 30 eggs, which hatch after about 8 months.
