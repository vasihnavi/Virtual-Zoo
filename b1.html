Bald Eagle

Haliaeetus leucocephalus

Did You Know?

Bald eagles can fly up to 30 mph and dive at speeds over 100 mph.

They have excellent vision, spotting prey from over a mile away.

Their nests, called aeries, are the largest of any bird species.

They were removed from the U.S. endangered species list in 2007.

Bald eagles are actually not bald; their white heads give them the name.

They symbolize strength and freedom in many cultures.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Wingspan of 6-7.5 feet.
Weight: 6.5 to 14 pounds.
Features: Dark brown body with a white head and tail; yellow beak and talons.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Primarily fish; also small mammals and carrion.
Hunting: Uses sharp talons and beak to catch and tear prey.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Found near large bodies of water, including lakes and coasts.
Range: North America, including the U.S. and Canada.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: 20-30 years in the wild.
Reproduction: Lays 1-3 eggs annually; nests in tall trees or cliffs.
