Giant Panda

Ailuropoda melanoleuca

Did You Know?

Giant pandas spend most of their time eating bamboo!

Despite their classification as carnivores, they are primarily herbivores.

Pandas can eat up to 40 kilograms (88 pounds) of bamboo each day.

They have a "pseudo-thumb," an adaptation to help grasp bamboo.

Giant pandas spend up to 12 hours a day eating bamboo.

They are solitary animals and prefer to live alone.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Males weigh 85 to 125 kg (187 to 276 lbs), females slightly smaller.
Distinctive Features: Black and white fur with a round face and large, black eye patches.
Teeth: Strong jaws and large molars for crushing bamboo.
Claws: Sharp claws to grip bamboo and climb trees.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Primarily bamboo, although they occasionally eat small mammals or birds.
Feeding: Spend 12 to 16 hours a day eating bamboo and can consume up to 40 kg (88 lbs) daily.
Water: Drink a lot of water to aid digestion, especially after eating bamboo.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Mountainous forests with bamboo forests.
Geography: Native to China, found in Sichuan, Shaanxi, and Gansu provinces.
Conservation: Classified as Vulnerable due to habitat loss and low reproduction rates.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: Live up to 20 years in the wild and over 30 years in captivity.
Reproduction: Females give birth to one or two cubs after a 5-month gestation period.
Social Structure: Solitary animals; mothers raise cubs alone for the first few years.
