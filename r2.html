Turtle

Testudines

Did You Know?

Some turtles can live over 100 years!

Sea turtles can hold their breath for up to 5 hours underwater.

Their shells are part of their skeleton and grow with them.

They have been around for over 200 million years, predating dinosaurs!

Some species of turtles can travel thousands of miles during migration.

Turtles don't have teeth but use sharp beaks to eat.

Know More About Us!

Physical Characteristics

Size: Varies by species; some are as small as 4 inches, while leatherback sea turtles grow up to 7 feet.
Weight: Ranges from a few pounds to over 1,500 lbs.
Features: Hard shell for protection, webbed feet, or flippers for swimming.

Diet and Feeding Habits

Diet: Herbivores, carnivores, or omnivores depending on species; common foods include seaweed, jellyfish, insects, and fruits.
Feeding: Adapted beaks help them eat a variety of foods.

Habitat and Distribution

Habitat: Oceans, freshwater rivers, ponds, and terrestrial areas.
Range: Found worldwide, from tropical seas to colder regions.

Lifespan and Reproduction

Lifespan: Varies by species; can live from 20 years to over a century.
Reproduction: Females lay eggs on land; hatchlings must make their way to the water.
Behavior: Exhibit natal homing, returning to the beach where they were born to lay eggs.
